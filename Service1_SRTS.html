Scientific Research and Technical
Services and Solutions

AIS provides our customers with the pragmatic science and environmental intelligence they need to maintain decision superiority. Through ecosystem studies, information services, and impact analyses we incorporate the entire ecosystem, including humans, into resource management decisions, giving our oceans the greatest chance to adapt and thrive.

Geonomics/eDNA

Genetics and genomics are powerful biological tools for studying various marine species and fish populations. We create novel techniques in genomics to help assess the presence / absence of species and their abundance in the hard to sample marine environment. This includes collecting eDNA samples in the field, processing them through, and performing analysis on bioinformatic data.

Protected Species Life Cycle Modeling

We analyze habitat data via field and remote sensing methods and analyze it to help develop life cycle models of protected species. A variety of data and tools help us understand the life cycles of protected species in order to best conserve them.

Ocean Engineering (Next Gen Aquaculture)

We support the design of next generation aquaculture gear intended to reduce entanglement risk of protected species through numerical modeling, stakeholder coordination and sensor deployment. Our work supports the development of a gear guide for aquaculture to help contribute towards standardizing aquaculture gear in support of regulatory needs. We also develop and define Aquaculture Opportunity Areas (AoA).

Ocean Acidification Research

AIS helps investigate the effects that ocean acidification will have on economically important species to aid in their management. We do this through laboratory experiments measuring differences in physiological responses in more acidic conditions as well as field data collection and data analysis.

Animal Husbandry

AIS supports the maintenance and operation of animal husbandry support systems. From microalgal cultures to spawning and larval rearing of target species, broodstock management, plumbing and seawater systems, managing electronic monitoring and control equipment as well as database structure and management we manage the complete hatchery lifecycle.

Plankton Research

The documentation of the life histories and population trends of individual species, and their interactions among multiple varied species is critical to our understanding of a dynamic environment and amid increasingly changing ocean conditions. We leverage microbiology best practices to identify, measure and quantify ichthyoplankton/zooplankton as well as participation in the at sea collection of plankton samples.

Marine Health and Disease Research

Together we our customers we gather and analyze information on the health of populations of shellfish by designing surveys, collecting samples, using molecular techniques to characterize disease, and analyzing data throughout the field season and in summary reports.

Education and Outreach

Stakeholder engagement on the latest scientific research and regulatory happenings is crucial to situational awareness. We coordinate with external partners, write scientific material for a broad array of audiences, update government websites and operate information helpdesks to support in the ever important knowledge transfer with the general public and various scientific communities.
